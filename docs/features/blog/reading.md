# Reading the blog

> **The blog** in PregnancyPal is a public list of published educational articles — each shown as a card with a title, a short excerpt, an author, a publish date, optional cover image, and tags — that you can open and read in full.

Pregnancy raises a lot of questions, and the blog is where PregnancyPal shares general, readable answers. It is a calm place to learn at your own pace: scroll the list, find something that speaks to where you are, and read the whole article without signing up for anything. It is open to everyone.

The blog is educational content. It is meant to inform and reassure, not to replace the personal guidance only your own provider can give.

## What you can do with the blog

- **Browse published articles** in a public list at `/blog`, each as a card.
- **Scan the essentials at a glance** — title, excerpt, author, publish date, and tags on every card.
- **See a cover image** where an article has one.
- **Open the full article** at `/blog/$postId` and read it as formatted text.
- **Share an article** using the share buttons on the read view.
- **Read without an account** — the blog is a public, read-only surface.

## How to read a blog article

1. **Open the blog**: go to the `/blog` route from the app.
2. **Browse the cards**: each card shows the title, excerpt, author, publish date, optional cover image, and tags.
3. **Pick an article** whose excerpt or tags match what you want to learn about.
4. **Tap the card** to open the full article at its own page (`/blog/$postId`).
5. **Read at your pace** — the article is presented as formatted text.
6. **Share it** with the share buttons if you want to pass it along to a partner, friend, or family member.

## Examples

- **A quick scan over coffee** — You open `/blog`, read a few excerpts, and spot an article whose tags match what is on your mind this week. You tap it, read the full piece, and feel a little more prepared.
- **Sharing with a partner** — An article explains something you both wondered about. You open it, tap a share button, and send it to your partner so you are on the same page.
- **Learning by topic** — You notice the tags on each card and follow the ones that interest you, building a sense of which subjects the blog covers without needing a search bar.

## Tips

- **Use the excerpt and tags to choose.** The short excerpt and the tags on each card are the fastest way to tell whether an article is what you want before you open it.
- **Note the publish date.** Pregnancy guidance can evolve, so the date on each card helps you judge how current a piece is.
- **Share the read view, not a screenshot.** The share buttons pass a proper link to the article so the person you send it to gets the full, formatted read.
- **Treat it as a starting point.** Articles are a great way to learn the vocabulary and the questions worth asking — then bring those questions to your provider.
- **Explore the deeper Education hub too.** For videos, knowledge-check quizzes, and interactive tools, visit the [Education hub](../content/quizzes-calculators).

## Frequently asked questions

### Do I need an account to read the blog?

No. The blog is a public, read-only surface at `/blog`. You can browse the cards and open any published article without signing in.

### Can I comment on blog posts?

No. Blog posts are read-only — there are no comments on them. The blog is for reading and sharing, not discussion.

### What information is on each blog card?

A title, a short excerpt, the author, the publish date, optional cover image, and tags. That gives you enough to decide whether to open the full article.

### How do I share an article?

Open the full article from its card, then use the share buttons on the read view to send the link.

### Is the blog medical advice for me specifically?

No. The articles are general pregnancy education for a broad audience. They are not tailored to your situation and are not a substitute for personal medical advice from your provider.

### Where do I find videos and quizzes instead?

Those live in the [Education hub](../content/quizzes-calculators), which also includes interactive tools like the [Kick Counter](../tools/kick-counter) and [Contraction Timer](../tools/contractions-timer).

## What this feature does NOT do

- **Does not give you personal medical advice.** Articles are general education written for everyone, not guidance about your specific pregnancy, symptoms, or test results.
- **Does not support comments or discussion.** Blog posts are read-only; there is no commenting on them.
- **Does not diagnose, treat, or predict anything.** Reading an article cannot tell you what is happening with your body or your baby.
- **Does not replace your provider's instructions.** If an article and your provider's advice differ, follow your provider.

**The PregnancyPal blog is educational reading only — it is not medical advice and is not specific to you. Always confirm anything that affects your care with your own healthcare provider before acting on it.**
