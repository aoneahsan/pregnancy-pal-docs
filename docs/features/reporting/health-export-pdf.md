# Export a health report PDF

> **Export Health Report** in PregnancyPal is a button that builds a downloadable PDF from your logged data — you choose which sections to include (vitals, appointments, weight, mood, symptoms) and an optional date range, and the app generates a tidy report you can share with your provider.

Appointments go faster when your provider can see your history at a glance. The Export Health Report feature turns the data you have been logging into a clean PDF you can bring to a visit, attach to a message, or print. You decide exactly what goes in it, so the report stays focused on what matters for that conversation.

The report is a record of what you logged — a way to share your own data, not a diagnosis or an interpretation of it.

## What you can do with the health report

- **Include or exclude Vitals** — recent blood pressure, heart rate, and temperature entries.
- **Include or exclude Appointments** — your scheduled and recorded appointments.
- **Include or exclude Weight** — your weight history.
- **Include or exclude Mood** — your mood tracking entries.
- **Include or exclude Symptoms** — your logged symptoms.
- **Set an optional date range** to limit the report to a window that matters.
- **Download a clean PDF** with a header, your patient information, and a table for each included section.

## How to generate your health report

1. **Open the export option**: find the **Export Health Report** button in the app.
2. **Choose your sections**: toggle which to include — Vitals (blood pressure, heart rate, temperature), Appointments, Weight, Mood, and Symptoms.
3. **(Optional) set a date range** to limit the report to a specific window, such as the last trimester.
4. **Generate the report**: PregnancyPal builds the PDF (using jsPDF) with a "PregnancyPal Health Report" header.
5. **Review the contents**: the PDF includes your patient information — name, email, current week, due date, and trimester — followed by a table for each section you included.
6. **Save and share**: the file downloads as `pregnancy-health-report-YYYY-MM-DD.pdf`, ready to bring to or send to your provider.

## Examples

- **Prepping for a check-up** — Before a visit you include **Vitals** and **Weight**, set the date range to the past month, and generate the PDF. You bring `pregnancy-health-report-2026-06-23.pdf` to the appointment so your provider can scan your recent readings quickly.
- **A focused mood conversation** — You want to talk about how you have been feeling, so you include only **Mood** and **Symptoms** and leave the rest out. The report stays short and on-topic for that discussion.
- **A full recent snapshot** — You include every section — Vitals, Appointments, Weight, Mood, and Symptoms — with no date range, producing a complete recent record of everything you have logged in those areas.

## Tips

- **Include only what the visit needs.** A report focused on Vitals and Weight is easier to discuss than one crammed with every section.
- **Use the date range to keep it relevant.** Limiting to the last few weeks usually beats a long history when you are preparing for a specific appointment.
- **Check the header details.** The PDF carries your name, email, current week, due date, and trimester — a quick glance confirms the snapshot is current before you share it.
- **The filename is dated for you.** Each export is `pregnancy-health-report-YYYY-MM-DD.pdf`, so keeping several versions stays organized automatically.
- **Log consistently for a fuller report.** The report can only show what you have entered — regular logging in [blood pressure](../tracking/blood-pressure), [weight](../tracking/weight), and [mood](../wellness/mood-tracking) makes it more useful.

## Frequently asked questions

### Which sections can the report include?

Vitals (blood pressure, heart rate, temperature — recent entries), Appointments, Weight history, Mood tracking, and Symptoms. You choose any combination of these.

### What is in the generated PDF?

A "PregnancyPal Health Report" header, your patient information (name, email, current week, due date, trimester), and a table for each section you chose to include.

### What is the file named?

It downloads as `pregnancy-health-report-YYYY-MM-DD.pdf`, with the date it was generated.

### Can I email or upload the report directly from the app?

No. The report is download-only — it saves to your device as a PDF. From there you can share it however you like, but the export itself does not email or upload it.

### Does the report include my diet plans or period data?

No. The report covers vitals, appointments, weight, mood, and symptoms only. It does not include diet plans, exercise logs, or period and fertility data.

### Is this report a diagnosis or assessment?

No. It is a record of the data you logged, formatted to share. It does not interpret your data, diagnose anything, or assess your pregnancy.

## What this feature does NOT do

- **Does not include everything in the app.** The report covers vitals, appointments, weight, mood, and symptoms only — not diet plans, exercise logs, or period and fertility data.
- **Does not email or upload itself.** It is download-only; you save the PDF and then share it manually.
- **Does not diagnose or interpret.** The report presents what you logged in tables — it draws no conclusions about your health.
- **Does not replace your medical records.** It is your personal summary to share, not an official clinical record or a substitute for what your provider keeps.

**The health report is a record of your own logged data to share — it is not a diagnosis, an assessment, or medical advice. Always review it together with your healthcare provider, who can interpret the numbers in the context of your full care.**
