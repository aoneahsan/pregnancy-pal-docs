# Sharing & PDF reports

> **Sharing** in PregnancyPal means two things: spreading the word about the app with a Share button (an in-app share window on the web, or the phone's native share sheet on Android), and sharing your own health data with a provider by exporting a PDF report.

There are two distinct kinds of "share" in PregnancyPal, and it helps to keep them separate. The first is sharing a link to PregnancyPal itself — telling a friend about the app, or posting it. The second is sharing your health information with someone who needs it, like your provider; for that, the PDF health report is the right tool, not a social share. This page covers both.

## What you can do with sharing

- **Share PregnancyPal on the web** through an in-app share window with social options — X, Facebook, LinkedIn, WhatsApp, Telegram, Reddit, and Email — plus a copy-link button.
- **Share from the Android app** using your phone's own native share sheet, the same menu you use across other apps.
- **Copy a link** to PregnancyPal to paste anywhere.
- **Export a health report PDF** of your logged data — vitals, appointments, weight, mood, symptoms, with an optional date range.
- **Hand the PDF to your provider** as a tidy record, which is the recommended way to share your health data.

## How sharing works

1. **Tap the Share button** wherever it appears in PregnancyPal.
2. **On the web**, an in-app share window opens with social buttons and a copy-link button. Pick a network to share to, or copy the link.
3. **On the Android app**, the phone's native share sheet opens instead, letting you share through any app you have installed.
4. **To share your health data**, don't use the social share — instead open the report export and generate a PDF.

### How to export and share a health report PDF

1. **Open the health report export** (see [Export a health report PDF](../reporting/health-export-pdf)).
2. **Choose the sections** to include — vitals, appointments, weight, mood, symptoms.
3. **Set an optional date range** so the report covers the period your provider cares about.
4. **Generate the PDF** and download it.
5. **Send or hand it to your provider** — by email, a patient portal, or in person at your appointment.

## Examples

- **Telling a friend on the web** — You tap Share on your laptop, the in-app window opens, you pick WhatsApp, and a message with the PregnancyPal link is ready to send.
- **Sharing from your Android phone** — You tap Share in the app and your familiar native share sheet appears; you send the link through whatever app you prefer.
- **Sharing data with your OB** — Before a checkup, you export a PDF covering the last month of weight, symptoms, and appointments, and bring it so your provider sees a clean summary instead of scrolling through the app.

## Tips

- **Use the PDF, not a social share, for health data.** Social sharing is for the app link; your private health information belongs in the report you give your provider.
- **Set a date range on the PDF** so the report is focused and easy to read.
- **Copy-link is always there on the web** if you'd rather paste the link yourself than pick a network.
- **The native share sheet on Android** lets you reach any installed app, including messaging and notes.
- **Double-check what's in the PDF before sending** so you're sharing exactly the sections you intend.

## Frequently asked questions

### What's the difference between the web share window and the native share sheet?

On the web, PregnancyPal shows its own in-app share window with social buttons (X, Facebook, LinkedIn, WhatsApp, Telegram, Reddit, Email) and a copy-link button. On the Android app, it opens your phone's native share sheet instead. Both are for sharing a link to PregnancyPal.

### How do I share my health data with my doctor?

Export a health report PDF and give that to your provider. The PDF is the intended way to share your logged data — see [Export a health report PDF](../reporting/health-export-pdf).

### Does the social share send my health data?

No. The share window and native share sheet share a link to PregnancyPal, not your private health entries. Your data is only shared when you deliberately export and send a PDF.

### Can I just copy a link instead of using a social network?

Yes. The web share window includes a copy-link button so you can paste the link wherever you want.

### Does sharing work on iPhone?

On the iOS web app, sharing uses the in-app web share window (with social options and copy-link), the same as any browser. There's no native iOS app — see [Using PregnancyPal on iPhone & iPad](./install-ios).

### Is the PDF report private?

The PDF is generated for you to download and share intentionally. Once you send it to someone, sharing is in your hands — only send it to people you trust, like your provider. Keep your account itself secure with [Security](../account/security).

## What this feature does NOT do

- **Social sharing does not send your health data** — it only shares a link to the PregnancyPal app.
- **PregnancyPal does not automatically send your data to your provider.** You choose to export a PDF and share it yourself.
- **The PDF report is a record you share manually**, not a live feed or a medical submission to any system.
- **PregnancyPal does not diagnose, interpret your data clinically, or replace prenatal care.** A report is information for you and your provider to discuss.

**PregnancyPal is a companion, not a clinician. For any medical question or concern about your pregnancy, contact your healthcare provider or your local emergency number. This app does not provide medical advice.**
