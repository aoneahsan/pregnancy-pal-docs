# Using PregnancyPal on iPhone & iPad

> **PregnancyPal on iOS** runs entirely as a web app in Safari — there is no native iPhone or iPad app on the App Store yet, so you open PregnancyPal in your browser and, if you like, Add it to your Home Screen for an app-like shortcut.

Let's be upfront: there's no native iOS app to download right now. That isn't a problem for getting things done — PregnancyPal's primary home is the web app, and every feature works in Safari on an iPhone or iPad exactly as it does on a phone or a laptop. You don't miss any tracking, profile, appointment, community, or content features by using the browser.

The one thing we want to be honest about: if you search the App Store for "PregnancyPal", you won't find a native app, because there isn't one yet. Use the web app instead, and optionally pin it to your Home Screen so it opens like an app.

## What you can do on iPhone & iPad

- **Use the full PregnancyPal web app** in Safari — sign in, track your pregnancy, log entries, browse content, and join the community.
- **Add to Home Screen** to get an icon that opens PregnancyPal in its own full-screen window, without the browser address bar.
- **Sign in to the same account** you use on Android or a desktop, so your data carries across.
- **Open normal page URLs** to land on specific sections (the web equivalent of deep links — see [Deep linking](./deep-linking)).
- **Share with Safari's tools** and export your data as a PDF to send to your provider (see [Sharing & PDF reports](./share-and-pdf)).

## How to add PregnancyPal to your Home Screen

1. **Open Safari** on your iPhone or iPad (this works best in Safari, Apple's built-in browser).
2. **Go to** [pregnancypal.aoneahsan.com](https://pregnancypal.aoneahsan.com).
3. **Sign in** or create your account — see [Create your account](../../getting-started/sign-up).
4. **Tap the Share button** in Safari (the square with an upward arrow, at the bottom on iPhone or the top on iPad).
5. **Scroll down and tap "Add to Home Screen".**
6. **Name the shortcut** (PregnancyPal is fine) and tap **Add**.
7. **Open PregnancyPal from the new Home Screen icon** — it now launches in its own full-screen window.

## Examples

- **First-time iPhone setup** — You open Safari, go to the web app, create an account, then Add to Home Screen so PregnancyPal sits next to your other apps. From then on it opens with one tap.
- **iPad on the couch** — You use the larger iPad screen to browse pregnancy content and review your dashboard, signed in to the same account as your phone.
- **No App Store confusion** — A friend tells you to "download the app". You explain there's no iOS app yet, open the web app together, and add it to her Home Screen in under a minute.

## Tips

- **Use Safari for the Home Screen shortcut.** "Add to Home Screen" is a Safari feature; other iOS browsers may not offer the same full-screen behavior.
- **Allow PregnancyPal to remember you** so you don't sign in every visit — keep the account secure with the guidance in [Security](../account/security).
- **Bookmark the web app too**, as a backup way in.
- **Notifications differ on iOS.** The native push reminders described for Android aren't available the same way through the web app — plan to check appointments and reminders inside the app.
- **Everything syncs through your account**, so switching between your iPhone web app and an Android phone later loses nothing.

## Frequently asked questions

### Is there a PregnancyPal app on the App Store?

No. There is no native iOS app yet. iPhone and iPad users use the web app at [pregnancypal.aoneahsan.com](https://pregnancypal.aoneahsan.com) in Safari. We don't want to imply otherwise.

### Do I lose any features by using the web app on iOS?

No. The tracking, profile, appointments, community, content, and export features all work in the browser. The native-only extras (push reminders, the `pregnancypal://` deep-link scheme, the native share sheet) are Android-app conveniences, not core features.

### Will my data sync if I later get an Android phone?

Yes. Your data lives in your account, not on the device. Sign in on any device and it's there.

### Does "Add to Home Screen" make it a real app?

It makes a convenient shortcut that opens PregnancyPal full-screen, which feels app-like. It is still the web app underneath — there's no separate iOS app being installed.

### Can I get appointment reminders on iPhone?

Open the app to see upcoming appointments and reminders. The push-notification system described for Android isn't delivered the same way to the iOS web app, so check inside the app rather than relying on phone notifications.

### Why isn't there an iOS app yet?

It simply hasn't been built yet. The web app covers iPhone and iPad fully in the meantime, which is why we point you there.

## What this feature does NOT do

- **There is no native iOS app** — nothing to download from the App Store, and we won't pretend there is.
- **The iOS web app does not deliver native push notifications** the way the Android app does; check reminders inside the app.
- **"Add to Home Screen" is not an installed app** — it's a shortcut to the web app.
- **PregnancyPal does not diagnose, monitor your health in the background, or replace prenatal care.** It's a tracking and organization companion.

**PregnancyPal is a companion, not a clinician. For any medical question or concern about your pregnancy, contact your healthcare provider or your local emergency number. This app does not provide medical advice.**
