# The PregnancyPal companion extension

> **The PregnancyPal companion extension** is a Chrome browser add-on that puts a quick slice of PregnancyPal — your pregnancy progress, a food-safety lookup, and fast health entry — into your browser toolbar, paired with your PregnancyPal account.

If you spend your day in a browser, the companion extension keeps PregnancyPal a single click away. It's not a replacement for the main app — it's a lightweight helper for the things you reach for most: checking how far along you are, looking up whether a food is generally considered safe in pregnancy, and jotting down a quick weight, mood, or symptom without switching tabs. Click the toolbar icon and a small popup opens; deeper settings live on its options page.

The extension is built for Chrome. It complements the web and Android apps and stays in sync with your account.

## What you can do with the extension

- **See pregnancy progress at a glance** — week X of 40, your trimester, and days remaining to your due date, with a quick link to open the full app.
- **Look up food safety** — type a food and see a status (Safe, Caution, Avoid, or Unknown), with your recent lookups remembered.
- **Log quick entries** — weight, mood, and symptoms, plus exercise and nutrition tips.
- **Reach settings, sign-out, help, and the version** from a More tab.
- **Pair with your account** using Chrome's built-in sign-in, set notification preferences and quiet hours, and configure sync on the options page.
- **Get reminder alarms and a right-click menu** (for example, "Check food safety") handled quietly in the background.

## How to set up the companion extension

1. **Add the PregnancyPal companion extension to Chrome.** It's a companion add-on for the Chrome browser.
2. **Click the toolbar icon** (you may want to pin it so it's always visible).
3. **Open the options page** — from the popup's More tab or by right-clicking the icon and choosing Options.
4. **Link your account** using Chrome's sign-in (Chrome Identity), so the extension knows whose pregnancy progress and data to show — details in [Options & settings](./options).
5. **Set your notification preferences and quiet hours** on the options page, if you want reminders.
6. **Open the popup** and explore its four tabs — see [The popup](./popup).

## Examples

- **Quick progress check** — Mid-morning you click the toolbar icon and the Home tab shows "Week 22 of 40, second trimester, 126 days to go", with a link to open the full app for more.
- **Food safety at lunch** — Deciding on a sandwich, you type a soft cheese into the Food Safety tab and get a Caution status, then confirm with your provider before deciding.
- **Logging without leaving your work** — You note a quick weight in the Health tab between meetings, and it syncs to your account so it's there in the app later.

## Tips

- **Pin the extension** so the toolbar icon is always one click away.
- **Link your account first** — most of the value (your real progress and synced entries) depends on signing in via Chrome Identity on the options page.
- **Treat the food-safety list as a starting point**, not the final word — it's a curated, non-exhaustive reference.
- **Use the popup for quick things and the full app for depth** — the extension links straight to the app when you need more.
- **Set quiet hours** so reminders don't arrive at bad times — see [Options & settings](./options).

## Frequently asked questions

### Which browsers does the extension support?

It's built for Chrome — it's the PregnancyPal companion extension for the Chrome browser.

### Is the extension a replacement for the app?

No. It's a companion — a lightweight helper for quick progress checks, food-safety lookups, and fast entries. The full experience lives in the web app at [pregnancypal.aoneahsan.com](https://pregnancypal.aoneahsan.com) and the [Android app](../mobile/install-android).

### How does the extension know my pregnancy details?

You link your PregnancyPal account using Chrome's sign-in (Chrome Identity) on the options page. Once paired, it shows your real progress and syncs your quick entries — see [Options & settings](./options).

### Is the food-safety list a medical database?

No. It's a curated, non-exhaustive reference meant as a helpful starting point. When in doubt about a food, confirm with your provider.

### Do I need the app installed to use the extension?

You need a PregnancyPal account to get personalized progress and sync. You sign in to that account from the extension's options page; you don't need the Android app specifically.

### Where can I download it?

It's the PregnancyPal companion extension for Chrome. We won't list a store link we can't verify here — add it to Chrome as the companion add-on for PregnancyPal.

## What this feature does NOT do

- **It is not a standalone medical tool or a full replacement for the app** — it's a companion helper.
- **The food-safety list is curated and non-exhaustive**, not a medical database, and can return "Unknown".
- **It does not diagnose, give medical advice, or confirm any food is safe for your specific pregnancy.**
- **It does not monitor your health in the background** or contact anyone on your behalf.
- **It is for Chrome**, and we make no claim about availability on other browsers.

**PregnancyPal is a companion, not a clinician. When in doubt about a food, symptom, or anything affecting your pregnancy, contact your healthcare provider or your local emergency number. This extension does not provide medical advice.**
