# Glucose tracking

> **Glucose tracking** in PregnancyPal is a fast log for blood-glucose readings — fasting, post-meal (1-hour, 2-hour), and ad-hoc — with reading-type categorization, trend charts, and PDF export so your provider can review your numbers across the day and across weeks.

If you've been diagnosed with gestational diabetes (or your provider is monitoring you for it), home glucose monitoring is part of your care. PregnancyPal makes the logging painless — type the number, tap the reading type, save. The trend view groups by reading type so you can see fasting trends separately from post-meal trends, which is the way most providers want to read the data.

## What you can do with glucose tracking

- **Log readings by type**: fasting, 1-hour post-breakfast / lunch / dinner, 2-hour post-meal, ad-hoc.
- **See trends per reading type** — fasting trend over time, separate from post-breakfast trend, etc.
- **Add notes** — what you ate, whether you exercised, how you felt.
- **Switch units** between mg/dL (US default) and mmol/L (UK / EU / Canada / India).
- **Get reminders** for the times your provider has asked you to test (e.g., morning fasting, 1-hour after breakfast).
- **Export as PDF** filtered by date range, with reading-type sections so your provider sees the structure they expect.

## How to log a glucose reading

1. **Open glucose tracking**: Dashboard → Vitals tile → Glucose. Or sidebar → Vitals → Glucose.
2. **Pick reading type**: Fasting, post-breakfast 1-hr, post-breakfast 2-hr, post-lunch 1-hr, post-lunch 2-hr, post-dinner 1-hr, post-dinner 2-hr, or ad-hoc. The picker is one tap.
3. **Enter the value**: Numeric input, defaults to your unit (mg/dL or mmol/L).
4. **Optional context**: Add a note ("oatmeal + berries", "skipped morning walk", "felt fine").
5. **Save**: The entry appears at the top of the log.
6. **Review trend**: Tap the chart to see readings grouped by type — fasting in one panel, post-meal types in their own panels.
7. **Export when ready**: For an appointment or to share, generate a PDF.

## Examples

- **Newly diagnosed GD** — At week 28 you're diagnosed with gestational diabetes. Your provider asks for fasting + 1-hour post-meal readings four times daily. You set reminders, log diligently, and bring the PDF to your week-30 follow-up. Your provider can scan the trend per type in seconds.
- **Diet-managed GD going well** — Your fasting numbers stay below 95 mg/dL, post-1-hour stays under 140 mg/dL. The trend chart shows steady patterns. Your provider continues with diet management, no medication needed yet.
- **Pre-existing type 2 diabetes** — You're already on insulin and being co-managed by an endocrinologist. PregnancyPal logs the same way; the export PDF lets you share with multiple providers.

## Tips

- **Test with clean, dry hands** to avoid contamination affecting readings.
- **Time post-meal readings precisely** — 1-hour means 60 minutes from the first bite, not 60 minutes from when you finished. Set the reminder for the right moment.
- **Note the meal** — your provider often asks "what did you eat with this number?". The note field saves a lot of memory work later.
- **Track exercise too**, separately. Walking after meals often lowers post-meal glucose; if you skipped your usual walk, the higher reading makes more sense.
- **Don't fixate on individual numbers.** Patterns matter. One occasional spike outside target is less informative than a week of consistent trends.

## Frequently asked questions

### What's the target range for pregnancy glucose?

Targets are clinical and your provider sets yours. Common general targets (per ACOG) for diet- or medication-managed gestational diabetes: fasting under 95 mg/dL (5.3 mmol/L), 1-hour postprandial under 140 mg/dL (7.8 mmol/L), 2-hour postprandial under 120 mg/dL (6.7 mmol/L). Your provider's targets may differ for your case.

### How often should I test?

Whatever your provider asked. Common GD protocols include 4 daily tests (fasting + 1-hour after each meal). Your provider may scale this up or down based on your numbers and overall management plan.

### Does PregnancyPal label readings "high" or "in range"?

No. Reading thresholds are clinical decisions, and what's "in range" depends on your provider's targets. The app shows your number against the trend; interpretation stays with your care team.

### Can I switch units?

Yes. Settings → Units → Glucose → mg/dL or mmol/L. Existing readings convert automatically.

### What if I forget to log right after testing?

Add the reading later — the time field is editable. The closer to the actual test time, the better, because retroactive logging can introduce small inaccuracies in pattern analysis.

### Can I track a continuous glucose monitor (CGM)?

PregnancyPal logs discrete readings, not continuous streams. CGM data export is on the roadmap. For now, you can manually log specific points if your provider asks for them.

### What happens if my numbers are consistently above targets?

That's a conversation with your provider. They may adjust your diet plan, add or change medication, or order additional monitoring. PregnancyPal will not alert or recommend — that's clinical territory.

### Does this integrate with the meal planner?

Indirectly. The [meal planner](../diet/condition-aware-plans) adjusts to gestational-diabetes-friendly suggestions if you've flagged GD in your pregnancy profile. The two features don't auto-correlate readings and meals, but the manual note field lets you tag what you ate.

## What this feature does NOT do

- **Diagnose gestational diabetes, type 1 or 2 diabetes, or any condition.** Diagnosis requires lab tests (glucose tolerance test, HbA1c) interpreted by a clinician.
- **Recommend medication or insulin doses.** Medication management is your provider's responsibility, full stop.
- **Replace lab-quality testing.** Home meters are reasonably accurate but vary; lab tests remain the reference for clinical decisions.
- **Send data to your provider automatically.** Sharing means exporting the PDF and bringing or sending it.
- **Replace your dietitian or diabetes educator.** PregnancyPal complements clinical care; it does not replace it.

**If your numbers are out of range and you don't have clear instructions from your provider, call them. Glucose management in pregnancy is more nuanced than a single threshold.**
