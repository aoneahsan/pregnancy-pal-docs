# Water intake tracking

> **Water intake tracking** in PregnancyPal is a one-tap hydration log — tap to add a glass, cup, milliliter, or ounce, watch the daily total climb toward your chosen target, and get gentle reminders so the day doesn't slip away with you forgetting to drink.

Hydration matters more in pregnancy than out of it — blood volume increases, amniotic fluid needs to be maintained, and dehydration is one of the most common triggers for Braxton-Hicks contractions and headaches. PregnancyPal's water tracker is designed to make hitting your target feel like a quiet daily rhythm, not a chore.

## What you can do with water intake tracking

- **Tap once per drink** — the default is "1 glass" (~250 ml / 8 oz), but you can change the increment.
- **Pick your unit** — glasses, cups, milliliters, or ounces. Settings apply globally.
- **Set a daily target** — choose from suggested ranges or enter your own.
- **Get gentle reminders** — choose intervals (every 1–3 hours) and quiet hours.
- **See today's total** prominently on the dashboard.
- **See history** — daily totals over a week, month, or full pregnancy.
- **Adjust quickly** if you mis-tap — subtract a glass with one button.

## How to log water

1. **Open the water tracker**: Dashboard → Wellness → Water. Or use the **Log water** quick-action tile (adds 1 glass instantly).
2. **Tap +1** for each drink. The day's total updates immediately.
3. **Change the increment if needed** — long-press the +1 button to switch between 1 glass, 1 cup, custom ml, custom oz.
4. **Adjust if you mis-tap** — tap the running total to reveal a small minus button.
5. **Review trends** — tap the chart icon for week / month / full-pregnancy views.
6. **Tweak reminders** — Settings → Water → reminder cadence + quiet hours.

## Examples

- **Steady daily rhythm** — You set a target of 10 glasses. Reminders fire every 90 minutes between 8 AM and 8 PM. By dinner, you've logged 7; an evening glass of water plus tea at dinner gets you to 10.
- **High-output day** — Hot day, walked more than usual. You drank closer to 13 glasses. The log shows the higher total; no judgment, just the number.
- **Travel day** — You forgot to log during a flight. Open the tracker when you land, tap +1 a few times to catch up, add a quick note "flight to NYC".

## Tips

- **Plain water is the goal**, but herbal teas, milk, and most other non-caffeinated drinks count toward hydration too. PregnancyPal lets you log them as "1 glass" without distinguishing types — keep it simple unless your provider asked for stricter tracking.
- **Caffeine doesn't count** toward your hydration target in most guidance. If you want, log it separately as a custom symptom or just don't include it.
- **The target is a guideline, not a number you must hit.** Some days will be lower; that's fine. The week's average is more meaningful than any single day.
- **Pair with bathroom timing** — frequent bathroom trips are a sign of adequate hydration. Very dark urine or fewer-than-typical trips can signal under-hydration.
- **Don't over-hydrate.** Drinking unusually large amounts of water in a short time isn't healthier; it can dilute electrolytes. The goal is steady throughout the day.

## Frequently asked questions

### How much water should I drink during pregnancy?

Most public guidance (ACOG, NHS) suggests around 8–12 cups (about 2–3 liters / 64–96 oz) of fluids daily during pregnancy, more in hot weather, during illness, or with increased physical activity. Your provider may give you a personalized target. PregnancyPal's default suggested target is 10 cups; adjust it to whatever your provider recommends.

### Do other beverages count?

Yes — water, herbal teas, milk, and most other non-caffeinated, non-sugary drinks contribute to hydration. Coffee, caffeinated tea, and energy drinks are typically not counted toward the target because of their mild diuretic effect, though moderate caffeine intake (under 200 mg/day per ACOG) is generally considered safe.

### What if I forget to log all day?

Catch up at the end of the day with an honest estimate. Five taps takes 3 seconds. Don't try to perfectly reconstruct — the rough trend is what's useful.

### Will the reminders be annoying?

You set the frequency. Default is every 90 minutes during waking hours, but you can stretch to every 3 hours, shorten to every hour, or disable entirely. Quiet hours stop all water notifications overnight.

### Can I change the glass size?

Yes. Long-press the +1 button to pick a different increment. Common options: 1 cup (240 ml), 1 glass (250 ml), 1 standard bottle (500 ml), custom (enter any ml or oz). The dashboard total uses your default; the log records the actual increment per tap.

### Should I count my morning coffee?

Caffeinated coffee is typically not counted toward hydration goals. If you want to log it for caffeine awareness, the [Symptom log](../tracking/symptoms) can record "caffeine — 1 cup" as a custom entry. Most guidance recommends keeping caffeine under about 200 mg/day in pregnancy.

### Does dehydration cause Braxton-Hicks contractions?

It can. Dehydration is a well-known trigger for practice contractions, especially in the third trimester. If you're feeling tight or noticing irregular tightenings, drinking a couple of glasses of water and resting on your left side often quiets them within an hour. If they persist or get regular, that's a different signal — see the [contractions timer](../tools/contractions-timer).

### Can I sync with a smart water bottle?

Smart-bottle integrations aren't shipped yet. Manual logging works for everyone; future updates may add automatic syncing with popular smart bottles.

## What this feature does NOT do

- **Diagnose dehydration.** Persistent thirst, dark urine, dizziness, or fast heart rate can be signs of dehydration that warrant medical attention — call your provider, not the app.
- **Replace your provider's hydration plan** for conditions like gestational hypertension or kidney issues that may require specific fluid management.
- **Auto-detect drinks.** PregnancyPal does not connect to smart bottles, scales, or fridge sensors today.
- **Differentiate beverage types automatically.** The log treats each tap equally; you decide what counts.
