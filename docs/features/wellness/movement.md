# Movement reminders

> **Movement reminders** in PregnancyPal nudge you to get up, stretch, or walk for a minute every hour during sitting-heavy days — a small habit that helps circulation, reduces swelling in the legs and feet, and slows the stiffness that builds during long sedentary stretches.

Movement is one of the most consistently recommended pregnancy habits, and not because anyone needs you to "work out". Even brief, gentle movement helps the cardiovascular system handle the extra blood volume of pregnancy, reduces fluid pooling that causes swollen ankles, and protects against the deep-vein-thrombosis risk that increases during pregnancy. Distinct from formal [exercise](./exercise), movement reminders are about *not staying still for too long*.

## What you can do with movement reminders

- **Get a reminder every 45–90 minutes** during active hours to get up and move briefly.
- **See a short cue** ("Stand up. Walk to the kitchen and back. Roll your ankles.").
- **Choose seated alternatives** if standing isn't possible (ankle circles, calf raises in your chair).
- **Skip a reminder** if you've just moved.
- **Customize active hours** so reminders don't fire when you're trying to rest.
- **Log a brief move** (optional) — adds a stamp to today's wellness summary.

## How to set up movement reminders

1. **Open movement**: Dashboard → Wellness → Movement.
2. **Enable the reminders** with the main toggle.
3. **Pick interval** — 45 / 60 / 90 minutes.
4. **Pick active hours** — typically your awake-and-active window.
5. **Choose cue style** — short cues, longer guided suggestions, or rotating tips.
6. **(Optional) enable the bed-rest mode** — replaces walks with seated/lying alternatives (ankle pumps, leg lifts, knee bends).

## Examples

- **Office day** — You sit for video calls most of the day. The 60-minute reminder fires; you walk to the kitchen for water (which doubles as a [water intake](./water-intake) cue), and you're back at your desk in 2 minutes.
- **Long-haul flight at 28 weeks** — Air travel increases DVT risk. Set the reminder interval to 45 minutes and stand or walk the aisle when each one fires. Calf raises in your seat between aisle walks help too.
- **Bed-rest day** — Bed-rest mode replaces walking cues with ankle pumps and gentle leg-bending cues. Even small calf-muscle activations support circulation.

## Tips

- **A minute is enough.** Movement reminders aren't asking for a workout — just a brief activation of your lower-body muscles to keep blood flowing.
- **Walk to water.** Pair movement with a glass of water. Two wellness habits in one trip.
- **Ankle pumps and calf raises** are the highest-value seated moves — they activate the calf-muscle pump that helps return blood to the heart.
- **Don't push when something hurts.** Pregnancy isn't the time for "no pain no gain". Discomfort is information; share it with your provider.
- **Reset position too.** Sometimes the right move is just standing for a minute, not walking. Listen to your body.

## Frequently asked questions

### Why is movement important in pregnancy?

Blood volume increases by roughly 30–50% during pregnancy, hormones soften veins (increasing varicose-vein and edema risk), and the gravid uterus presses on major veins in the abdomen and pelvis (slowing return blood flow from the legs). Combined, these changes raise the risk of swelling, varicose veins, and deep vein thrombosis (DVT). Brief, regular movement counters all three by keeping blood flowing through the calf-muscle pump.

### Is sitting all day dangerous in pregnancy?

"Dangerous" is too strong for most people, but prolonged sitting (especially without breaks) does increase swelling and discomfort, and modestly increases DVT risk in higher-risk groups. The fix is simple: get up regularly. PregnancyPal's reminders make that habit easy.

### What about long flights or car rides?

Long-haul travel during pregnancy increases DVT risk. Common recommendations: move every 30–60 minutes (walk the aisle, stop the car for a short walk), do ankle pumps and calf raises while seated, stay well hydrated, and consider compression stockings — especially in the third trimester. Talk to your provider before long trips.

### Should I count steps?

PregnancyPal doesn't currently sync with phones' step counters or fitness wearables. If steps motivate you, use your existing tracker alongside; otherwise, the movement reminder approach (frequency over total) often works better for the goal of avoiding long sedentary stretches.

### What if my provider has me on bed rest?

Enable bed-rest mode in movement settings. The reminders switch to seated/lying alternatives (ankle pumps, leg-bending) that maintain circulation without requiring standing or walking. Follow your provider's specific bed-rest instructions over any generic suggestion.

### Can I combine movement with stretching?

Yes, and many people do. A 60-second cat-cow, gentle pelvic tilts, or seated forward folds can be a movement reset and a stretch in one. See [exercise](./exercise) for more.

## What this feature does NOT do

- **Detect actual movement.** No accelerometer, no fitness-tracker sync. The reminder is time-based.
- **Replace exercise.** Movement reminders are about avoiding prolonged stillness; structured exercise is a separate, complementary habit.
- **Prevent DVT.** Reducing risk and preventing aren't the same. If you have specific DVT risk factors (history, prolonged immobilization, certain conditions), your provider may recommend additional measures.
- **Recommend medical treatment** for swelling, varicose veins, or any specific symptom.

**Sudden swelling — especially asymmetric (one leg much more than the other), accompanied by warmth, redness, or pain — can be a sign of DVT and warrants an urgent call to your provider, regardless of how often you've been moving.**
