# Medications tracking

> **Medications tracking** in PregnancyPal is a simple log for prescriptions and supplements during pregnancy — name, dose, schedule, reminders, and a clean history — so you never forget your prenatal vitamin again and you have a complete list ready when a new provider asks "what are you taking?"

Most pregnant people take at least a prenatal vitamin, and many take additional prescriptions or supplements (iron, calcium, vitamin D, thyroid medication, etc.). Keeping the list straight matters — providers ask about it at every visit, hospital intake will require it during delivery, and remembering doses through first-trimester fatigue is genuinely hard. PregnancyPal does the boring memory work so you don't have to.

## What you can do with medications tracking

- **Add medications and supplements** — name, dose, route (oral, topical, injectable), schedule.
- **Get reminders** at the times you specify (once daily, twice daily, every X hours, custom).
- **Log a dose taken** with one tap when the reminder fires, or manually any time.
- **See adherence history** — did you take it yesterday? last week?
- **Add notes per dose** — taken with food, slight nausea after, etc.
- **Mark a medication as discontinued** when you stop — keeps the history without cluttering the active list.
- **Export the active list as PDF** for new providers or hospital intake.

## How to add a medication

1. **Open medications**: Dashboard → Wellness → Medications. Or sidebar → Wellness → Medications.
2. **Tap Add medication**.
3. **Enter the name** — start typing and pick from common pregnancy medications/supplements, or enter a custom name.
4. **Enter dose** — e.g., "1 tablet", "500 mg", "10 units". Free text so it matches what your provider wrote.
5. **Pick route** — oral, sublingual, topical, injection, etc.
6. **Set schedule** — once daily, twice daily, every 8 hours, custom times.
7. **Set reminders** — toggle on/off; reminders fire as push notifications at scheduled times.
8. **Save** — the medication appears in your active list with the next dose time.
9. **Log doses as they happen** — tap the medication when the reminder fires (or any time) to mark "taken".

## Examples

- **Prenatal vitamin** — Name: "Prenatal multivitamin". Dose: "1 tablet". Schedule: once daily at 8 AM. The 8 AM reminder fires; you take the pill and tap **Taken**. Adherence stays at 100% for the week.
- **Iron supplement for anemia** — Your provider prescribes 65 mg elemental iron, twice daily with food. You log both doses, set reminders at 8 AM and 6 PM, and add a per-dose note when iron causes mild nausea. The PDF export shows your provider the schedule you actually followed.
- **Thyroid medication** — You've been on levothyroxine for years. Pregnancy doesn't change the medication but dose may be adjusted by your endocrinologist. You log every morning dose. When dose changes, you mark the old entry "discontinued" and add the new one — the history shows the transition.

## Tips

- **Add medications as your provider prescribes them**, not retroactively from memory. Catch-up logging is fine but the schedule is most accurate when set up on day one.
- **Be specific with names.** "Prenatal" is fine; "Prenatal multivitamin, brand X" is better. If you change brands later, mark the old one discontinued and add the new one.
- **Note any side effects** in the dose-level note field. Nausea, fatigue, stomach upset — these are useful data points for the provider conversation.
- **Keep prenatal vitamins on the list even if they feel routine.** Hospital intake will ask; having the list ready saves time and avoids the "I take a prenatal but I forget which one" answer.
- **Don't add medications PregnancyPal didn't prescribe.** This tool is for tracking *your* provider's plan, not for suggesting or modifying it.

## Frequently asked questions

### Does PregnancyPal check for drug interactions?

No. PregnancyPal is not a drug-interaction checker, a pharmacy database, or a clinical decision support tool. Interactions between medications, supplements, and pregnancy require a pharmacist or your provider — not an app log. Always tell every provider you see about every medication and supplement you take.

### Is it safe to take [drug X] during pregnancy?

PregnancyPal does not answer that question. Safety in pregnancy depends on the specific drug, dose, gestational age, your medical history, and trade-offs your provider knows. Resources like the FDA pregnancy categories, LactMed for breastfeeding, and your provider's judgment are the right tools — not an app's opinion.

### Can I track over-the-counter medications too?

Yes — anything you put into your body that you want to log. Common pregnancy OTC entries: acetaminophen (Tylenol), antacids, magnesium for cramps. Always check with your provider before starting any OTC during pregnancy, because what's safe in general isn't always safe in your specific case.

### What if I miss a dose?

Just tap "Taken late" or skip and log the next one. The adherence chart accounts for missed doses honestly. Don't double-up to compensate unless your provider told you to — that's a medical question, not an app one.

### Can I share the list with my provider?

Yes — open Medications → Export as PDF → choose "active only" or "include history". The PDF lists name, dose, route, schedule, start date, and (if applicable) discontinued date. Bring it to appointments or send it ahead.

### Does PregnancyPal sync with my pharmacy?

No. There's no integration with pharmacies, electronic health records, or pill bottles. Manual entry is the model.

### Can I add a medication for someone else?

The current design is single-user. The medications list is your medications, period. There's no "family member" tracking inside PregnancyPal.

### What about herbal supplements?

You can log them — they're just custom-name entries. But be cautious: many herbal supplements are not well-studied in pregnancy and some are explicitly unsafe. Tell your provider about every herbal you take, just like prescriptions.

### Will reminders work offline?

Reminders are scheduled locally on your device, so they fire even without a connection. The "taken" log syncs to your account when you reconnect.

## What this feature does NOT do

- **Check drug interactions.** Not a clinical safety tool. Talk to your pharmacist and provider.
- **Recommend medications.** PregnancyPal does not suggest "you might want to take X" — every medication on your list is one *you and your provider* decided on.
- **Modify dosing.** Don't change a dose because an app told you to. Don't.
- **Diagnose adherence problems.** Low adherence shown in the chart is a fact, not a finding. If you're missing doses because of side effects, talk to your provider about adjusting.
- **Replace your pharmacy's labeling.** Always follow the label and any specific instructions your prescriber gave.

**If you're considering starting, stopping, or changing any medication during pregnancy, talk to your provider first. PregnancyPal will not substitute that conversation.**
