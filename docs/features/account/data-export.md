# Getting a copy of your data

> **Getting a copy of your data** in PregnancyPal today means generating the **Health Report PDF** — a document that gathers your tracked vitals, appointments, weight, mood, and symptoms into one file you can save or share.

Many people want to take their journey out of the app — to bring numbers to an appointment, keep a personal record, or simply have a copy in hand. In PregnancyPal, the way to do that right now is the Health Report PDF. It pulls together the information you've logged into a single, readable document.

This page explains what the report includes, how to create one, and — just as importantly — what it does and doesn't cover, so you know exactly what you're getting.

## What you can do with the Health Report PDF

- **Pull your tracked health data into one document** instead of scrolling through screens.
- **Save a copy** for your own records.
- **Share it** with your healthcare provider, partner, or anyone you choose.
- **Bring real numbers to appointments** — trends are easier to discuss on paper.
- **Keep a snapshot in time** of where things stand.

The report focuses on the health information you've tracked: **vitals, appointments, weight, mood, and symptoms.**

## How to generate your data copy

1. **Open the report tool**: head to the Health Report PDF in PregnancyPal. See the full walkthrough on the [Health Report PDF](../reporting/health-export-pdf) page.
2. **Choose what to include**, if the report offers selections, and confirm the range you want.
3. **Generate the PDF**: the app assembles your vitals, appointments, weight, mood, and symptoms into a document.
4. **Save or share it**: keep the file, or send it to your provider or a family member.
5. **Repeat any time**: generate a fresh report whenever you want an updated snapshot — there's no limit.

## Examples

- **Prenatal visit prep** — Before a check-up, you generate a Health Report PDF so your weight trend, recent symptoms, and blood-pressure readings are all in one place to discuss with your provider.
- **A personal keepsake** — You like having a record of your journey, so every trimester you generate a report and save it alongside your photos and notes.
- **Sharing with your partner** — Your partner travels for work and wants to stay in the loop. You generate a report and email it so they can see how things are going.

## Tips

- **Set your name first.** Update your [profile](./profile) before generating a report so it's labeled correctly.
- **Generate fresh copies as needed.** The report reflects your data at the moment you create it, so make a new one when you want the latest.
- **Bring it to appointments.** A printed or on-screen PDF makes it easy to walk a provider through your trends.
- **Keep your own backup.** Saving the PDF gives you a personal copy outside the app.
- **Know the scope.** The report covers vitals, appointments, weight, mood, and symptoms — see the limitations below for what it doesn't include.

## Frequently asked questions

### What's included in the data copy?

The Health Report PDF includes your tracked **vitals, appointments, weight, mood, and symptoms.** It's the way to get a copy of that data out of the app today.

### Is there a full account data download?

No. There is no separate, complete account data-dump download. The Health Report PDF is the way to get a copy of your tracked health data right now. It is not a complete export of every field in your account.

### Can I share the report with my provider?

Yes. Once generated, the PDF is yours to save or share with your provider, partner, or anyone you choose.

### How often can I generate a report?

As often as you like. Each report reflects your data at the time you create it, so generate a new one whenever you want an up-to-date snapshot.

### Does the report include my community posts or messages?

No. The Health Report PDF focuses on tracked health data — vitals, appointments, weight, mood, and symptoms — not community activity, settings, or profile fields.

### Will the report make any medical interpretation of my data?

No. It's a record of what you logged. It presents your data; it does not analyze, score, or diagnose anything.

### Where can I learn more about generating the PDF?

The dedicated [Health Report PDF](../reporting/health-export-pdf) page has the full step-by-step walkthrough.

## What this feature does NOT do

- It does **not** provide a complete account data dump. It covers your tracked vitals, appointments, weight, mood, and symptoms — not every field stored in your account.
- It does **not** include your profile details, app settings, or community activity.
- It does **not** interpret, score, or diagnose your data. The report is a record, not an assessment.
- It does **not** send itself to your provider automatically — sharing is always your choice.
- It is **not** a clinical document. Your provider's records remain the authoritative source for your care.

**PregnancyPal is a personal organizing companion, not medical advice. The Health Report PDF is a copy of what you tracked, not a medical evaluation. Always review your health with your healthcare provider, who can interpret your data in the context of your care.**
