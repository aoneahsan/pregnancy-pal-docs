# Joining community cohorts

> **Community cohorts** in PregnancyPal are groups you can browse by category, join, or leave — gathering people at a similar stage, such as a due-date month, the trying-to-conceive journey, a trimester, or a health topic. They're spaces for peer support and shared experience, not sources of medical advice.

There's something steadying about knowing other people are a few days ahead of you, or going through the exact week you're in. The communities list lets you find those groups, see how many members each has, and join the ones that fit. Once you've joined, their posts flow into your Feed. It's company for the journey — fellow travelers, not professionals.

## What you can do with cohorts

- **Browse the communities list** and filter by category: **Due-Date**, **Trying-to-Conceive**, **Trimester**, **Health**, or **General**.
- **See member counts** so you know how active a group is before joining.
- **Join or leave a group** with the Join/Leave Group button on each community.
- **Read your Feed** — the searchable, filterable stream of posts from the communities you've joined.
- **Check My Activity** to see your own posts in one place.
- **Search across posts** by content, tags, or author names.

## How to find and join a cohort

1. **Open the Community feature** (route `/community`).
2. **Browse the communities list**, or **filter by category** — Due-Date, Trying-to-Conceive, Trimester, Health, or General.
3. **Look at the member count** on each group to gauge how active it is.
4. **Tap "Join Group"** on a community that fits your stage or interest.
5. **Open the Feed tab** to read posts from the communities you've joined.
6. **Leave anytime** by tapping the same button, now showing "Leave Group", if a group isn't for you.

## Examples

- **A due-date group** — You join the cohort matching your due-date month and suddenly your Feed fills with people counting down the same weeks, comparing notes on the same milestones.
- **A trying-to-conceive cohort** — Before pregnancy, you join the Trying-to-Conceive group to follow others' experiences while you track your cycle in the [Period](../period/cycle-tracking) and [Fertility](../fertility/conception-window) features.
- **A health-topic group** — You filter to the Health category and join a group focused on a topic you're navigating, then use search to find earlier posts about it.

## Tips

- **Pick a couple of groups, not all of them.** A focused Feed from two or three relevant cohorts is more readable than a firehose from everything.
- **Use member counts as a rough activity signal.** A larger group often means more posts and faster replies, while a smaller one can feel more personal.
- **Lean on the matching cohort for your stage.** A due-date or trimester group keeps the conversation relevant to where you actually are.
- **Search before asking.** The search spans post content, tags, and author names, so your question may already be answered.
- **Leave freely.** Joining and leaving is reversible; curate your Feed as your needs change.

## Frequently asked questions

### What categories of communities are there?

Communities are organized into **Due-Date**, **Trying-to-Conceive**, **Trimester**, **Health**, and **General**. You can filter the list by these categories to find a group that matches your stage or interest.

### What does joining a group actually do?

Joining adds that community's posts to your Feed and lets you post in it. The Join/Leave Group button toggles your membership, and the member count reflects how many people have joined.

### Can I be in more than one cohort at a time?

Yes. You can join multiple communities, and their posts all flow into your single Feed, which you can search and filter.

### Why do week or cohort communities help?

Being grouped with people at the same stage means the conversation is relevant — the same milestones, the same questions, the same timeline. It's easier to feel understood and to find posts that apply to you right now.

### How do I leave a community?

Open the community and tap the button that now reads "Leave Group". You can rejoin later; nothing is permanent.

### Where do my own posts show up?

Your posts appear in the My Activity tab, so you can find everything you've shared in one place. See [posting and visibility](./posting-and-anonymous) for how posting works.

### Is anything I read here medical advice?

No. Communities are peer spaces. Members share personal experiences, which can be supportive but are not professional guidance.

## What this feature does NOT do

- **It does not provide medical advice.** Cohorts are peer support; members are not clinicians, and their experiences may not apply to you.
- **It does not vet or verify** what other members say. Treat shared information as personal anecdote, not fact.
- **It does not match you to care** or replace any part of your prenatal care team.
- **It does not screen members or guarantee safety.** Use your own judgment about what you read and whom you trust.

**PregnancyPal communities are peer support, not medical advice and not a substitute for professional care. For any health question, decision, or concern raised in a group, please consult your healthcare provider — only a qualified clinician can advise on your specific situation.**
