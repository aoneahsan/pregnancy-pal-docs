# Meal planning

> **Meal planning** in PregnancyPal is a ready-made daily diet plan built around your trimester, your dietary restrictions, your allergies, and a calorie target you pick. Each plan lists meals with times, recipes, preparation steps, tips, and macronutrients. The plans are general educational suggestions — not personalized medical nutrition therapy.

Eating well during pregnancy feels harder than it should. You have new cravings, foods you suddenly can't stand, and a long list of "should I eat this?" questions. PregnancyPal's meal planning gives you a simple starting point: a full day of meals you can look at, follow, or adapt.

The plan is a helpful template, not a prescription. It reflects the preferences you enter and the general nutrition needs of pregnancy. Your provider or a registered dietitian always knows your situation better than an app does. Use the plan as a guide, and bring any real nutrition questions to them.

## What you can do with meal planning

- **View your Active Diet Plan** — a full day of meals laid out for you.
- **Open any meal** to see its time, name, type, calorie count, numbered preparation instructions, and tips.
- **Check the macros** for each meal — protein, carbs, and fat.
- **Tune the plan** through the Edit Preferences dialog (restrictions, allergies, meal preferences, calorie target, and notes).
- **See your Nutrition Targets** — a grid of six daily targets shown as ranges.
- **See a Hydration Reminder** card alongside your meals.
- **Review Foods to Avoid**, split into High Risk and Limit categories.
- **Regenerate the plan** after you change your preferences so it matches your inputs.

## How to view and adjust your diet plan

1. **Open your plan**: Dashboard → Diet Plan (the `/diet-plan` screen).
2. **Read the Active Diet Plan** — meals appear in order with their scheduled times.
3. **Tap a meal** to expand its preparation instructions, tips, and macros (protein / carbs / fat).
4. **Tap "Edit Preferences"** to open the preferences dialog.
5. **Set your inputs** in the dialog:
   - **Dietary Restrictions** — check any that apply (vegetarian, vegan, gluten-free, dairy-free, nut-free, halal, kosher, low-sodium).
   - **Allergies** — type them as a comma-separated list.
   - **Meal Preferences** — toggle breakfast, lunch, dinner, and snacks on or off.
   - **Daily Calorie Target** — pick from the dropdown (1800, 2000, 2200, 2400, or 2600, labeled Light to Very Active activity).
   - **Additional Notes** — add anything else you want reflected.
6. **Save** — the plan regenerates around your trimester, restrictions, and calorie target.

## Examples

- **Vegetarian at 2200 calories** — You check "vegetarian" and set the target to 2200. Your plan fills with plant-forward meals: a breakfast bowl, a lentil lunch, and a paneer or bean dinner, each showing its protein, carbs, and fat so you can see where the protein comes from.
- **Dairy-free with a nut allergy** — You check "dairy-free" and type "peanuts, tree nuts" in Allergies. The regenerated plan steers around dairy and nuts, and the Foods to Avoid card still reminds you of general high-risk pregnancy foods.
- **Skipping snacks** — You toggle "snacks" off in Meal Preferences. The plan rebuilds with three meals instead of four, redistributing the calories across breakfast, lunch, and dinner.

## Tips

- **Start with the calorie target your provider suggests.** The dropdown labels (Light to Very Active) are general activity bands, not a personalized prescription.
- **Use the prep steps as inspiration.** Swap an ingredient you dislike for a similar one — the plan is a template, not a rule.
- **Re-open Edit Preferences whenever things change.** New aversion in the first trimester? A craving in the third? Update your inputs and regenerate.
- **Read the macros, not just the calories.** Pregnancy raises protein needs, so the protein number per meal is worth a glance.
- **Pair the plan with your water log.** The Hydration Reminder card sits next to your meals for a reason.
- **Don't force a meal that makes you queasy.** Nausea is real; eat what stays down and talk to your provider if you can't keep food down.

## Frequently asked questions

### How does PregnancyPal build my plan?

It combines your current trimester with the inputs you set in Edit Preferences — your dietary restrictions, allergies, meal toggles, and calorie target. It then assembles meals that fit those constraints. It does not analyze lab results, weight history, or any medical record.

### Can I change the plan after it generates?

Yes. Tap "Edit Preferences" anytime, change your inputs, and save. The plan regenerates to match. There is no limit on how often you can regenerate.

### Are the recipes and portions exact?

Treat them as guidance. Calories and macros are estimates for a typical serving. Real portions vary by ingredient brand and how you cook. Use the numbers to compare meals, not as precise medical values.

### Does the plan account for twins or a specific medical condition?

No. The plan reflects only the preferences you enter. It does not adjust for multiples, gestational diabetes, preeclampsia, anemia, or any other condition. See [condition-aware plans](./condition-aware-plans) for the honest boundary.

### What if I have a food allergy not in the restriction list?

Type it in the **Allergies** free-text field as a comma-separated entry. The plan uses that text when it regenerates. Always double-check ingredient lists yourself — an app can't guarantee an allergen-free meal.

### Where do the protein, carbs, and fat numbers come from?

Each meal carries its own macro values for protein, carbs, and fat. They're shown so you can understand the balance of a meal, not as a target you must hit exactly.

### Is this plan enough on its own?

No. It's a companion to prenatal care, not a replacement for it. Your provider and, where needed, a registered dietitian should guide your actual nutrition.

## What this feature does NOT do

- **Does not provide medical nutrition therapy.** Plans are general educational suggestions, not a personalized clinical diet.
- **Does not generate condition-specific plans** for gestational diabetes, preeclampsia, anemia, or any other diagnosis.
- **Does not diagnose, treat, or predict** any pregnancy outcome or nutrient deficiency.
- **Does not guarantee an allergen-free meal** — always read ingredient labels yourself.
- **Does not track what you actually ate** — it shows a suggested plan, not a food diary.
- **Does not replace lab work or provider monitoring** of your nutrition or weight.

**PregnancyPal's diet plans are for general education only and are not medical advice. Your prenatal needs are unique — always consult your healthcare provider or a registered dietitian before making nutrition decisions, especially if you have any pregnancy complication, allergy, or chronic condition.**
