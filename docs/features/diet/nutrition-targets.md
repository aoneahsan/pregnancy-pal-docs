# Nutrition targets

> **Nutrition targets** in PregnancyPal are a small grid of six daily nutrient ranges — protein, calcium, iron, folate, fiber, and vitamin D — shown next to your diet plan, along with a calorie target you choose and a hydration reminder. They are general educational ranges, not a personalized medical prescription.

When you're pregnant, a few nutrients matter more than usual. PregnancyPal surfaces six of them as easy-to-read ranges so you have a general sense of what a balanced pregnancy day looks like. It pairs that with a calorie target you set yourself and a gentle reminder to drink water.

These ranges are general. Your exact needs depend on your body, your trimester, your activity, and any condition you have. Think of the targets as a reference to discuss with your provider — not as numbers you must hit precisely or strict limits you must never cross.

## What you can do with nutrition targets

- **See six nutrition targets** shown as min–max ranges:
  - **Protein** (g)
  - **Calcium** (mg)
  - **Iron** (mg)
  - **Folate** (mcg)
  - **Fiber** (g)
  - **Vitamin D** (IU)
- **Pick a Daily Calorie Target** from the dropdown — 1800, 2000, 2200, 2400, or 2600, labeled Light to Very Active activity.
- **Read the Hydration Reminder** card that sits with your plan.
- **See the targets update** in context as you change your trimester and preferences.

## How to view targets and set your calories

1. **Open your plan**: Dashboard → Diet Plan.
2. **Find the "Nutrition Targets" grid** — six cards, each showing a min–max range.
3. **Read each range** to get a general feel for the day (for example, a protein range rather than a single number).
4. **Tap "Edit Preferences"** to change your calorie level.
5. **Pick a Daily Calorie Target** from the dropdown that matches the activity band your provider suggested.
6. **Save** — your plan and calories adjust together.
7. **Check the Hydration Reminder** card for a nudge to keep drinking water through the day.

## Examples

- **Reading the protein range** — Your Protein card shows a min–max band. You glance at the protein number on each meal in your plan and see they add up toward that range across the day.
- **Choosing a calorie band** — Your provider says you're moderately active. You open Edit Preferences and pick 2200 from the dropdown. The plan rebuilds around that level.
- **Using the iron and folate cards together** — You note your Iron and Folate ranges, then bring them up at your next visit alongside any supplement your provider recommended, like a prenatal vitamin.

## Tips

- **Use the ranges as a compass, not a scorecard.** Hitting "roughly in range over the week" matters more than any single day.
- **Match the calorie dropdown to your real activity.** When unsure, ask your provider which band fits you.
- **Pair nutrition targets with the meal macros.** The protein/carbs/fat on each meal show how the day adds up.
- **Folate and iron deserve a provider chat.** Many people take a prenatal vitamin; your provider decides what you need.
- **Keep the hydration card in view.** Water needs rise in pregnancy, and the reminder is there to help.
- **Don't chase vitamin D from food alone.** It's hard to get enough from diet; your provider may suggest a supplement.

## Frequently asked questions

### Where do these ranges come from?

They are general educational ranges for pregnancy nutrition shown to give you context. They are not calculated from your blood work, and they are not a personal prescription.

### Why ranges instead of exact numbers?

Because needs vary from person to person and day to day. A range is a more honest, less stressful way to show "about this much" than a single target you'd feel pressured to hit exactly.

### How do I change my calorie target?

Open Edit Preferences and pick a new value from the Daily Calorie Target dropdown (1800 to 2600). Save, and your plan regenerates at that level.

### Are these the only nutrients that matter?

No. Pregnancy nutrition involves many nutrients. PregnancyPal highlights six common ones for a quick reference — it isn't a complete nutrition assessment.

### Should I take supplements to reach the ranges?

That's a question for your provider. Many people take a prenatal vitamin, but doses and needs are individual. Don't start or change supplements based on the app alone.

### Does the hydration reminder track how much I drink?

The Hydration Reminder card here is a nudge. To actually log water, use the dedicated [water intake tracking](../wellness/water-intake) feature.

### Can I exceed a range?

The ranges are guidance, not hard limits. If you're worried about getting too much or too little of anything, talk to your provider.

## What this feature does NOT do

- **Does not set personalized medical nutrient targets** — the ranges are general education.
- **Does not measure your actual intake** or tell you if you met a target.
- **Does not test your blood, iron, or vitamin levels.**
- **Does not diagnose, treat, or predict** any deficiency or pregnancy condition.
- **Does not recommend specific supplement doses** — that's your provider's role.
- **Does not replace lab work or clinical monitoring** of your nutrition.

**PregnancyPal's nutrition targets are general educational ranges and are not medical advice. Your real needs depend on your body, trimester, and health — always consult your healthcare provider or a registered dietitian before changing your diet, calories, or supplements.**
