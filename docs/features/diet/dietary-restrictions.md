# Dietary restrictions

> **Dietary restrictions** in PregnancyPal are the food rules and allergies you set so your diet plan skips foods you can't or won't eat. Alongside them, the app shows a general "Foods to Avoid" reference for pregnancy, split into High Risk and Limit categories. This reference is educational, not personalized medical advice.

Pregnancy comes with a lot of food questions. Some are about your own choices — vegetarian, halal, low-sodium. Others are about safety — which foods are commonly limited during pregnancy. PregnancyPal lets you record the first kind so your plan respects them, and shows you the second kind as general, easy-to-read guidance.

Food-safety advice is general here, not personal. Guidelines vary by country and by your own health. Always treat the Foods to Avoid list as a starting point for a conversation with your provider, not as the final word for your specific pregnancy.

## What you can do with dietary restrictions

- **Check standard restrictions** — vegetarian, vegan, gluten-free, dairy-free, nut-free, halal, kosher, and low-sodium.
- **Enter allergies** as comma-separated free text so the plan steers around them.
- **See your restrictions reflected** in your regenerated diet plan.
- **Review "Foods to Avoid"** in two clear groups: High Risk and Limit.
- **Adjust anytime** — your choices aren't permanent and can change each trimester.

## How to set restrictions and allergies

1. **Open your plan**: Dashboard → Diet Plan.
2. **Tap "Edit Preferences"** to open the preferences dialog.
3. **Check your Dietary Restrictions** — tick every box that applies (you can pick more than one, like vegetarian + low-sodium).
4. **Type your Allergies** in the free-text field, separated by commas — for example, `peanuts, shellfish, eggs`.
5. **Save** — the plan regenerates and avoids the foods you flagged.
6. **Scroll to "Foods to Avoid"** on the main plan view to read the High Risk and Limit guidance.

## Examples

- **Halal + nut allergy** — You check "halal" and type "peanuts, almonds" in Allergies. The regenerated plan avoids non-halal items and those nuts, while the Foods to Avoid card still reminds you about general high-risk foods.
- **Vegan switch** — You move from vegetarian to vegan mid-pregnancy. You uncheck nothing extra — just check "vegan" and save. The plan rebuilds without animal products, and you bring up B12 and iron with your provider.
- **Low-sodium request** — Your provider mentions watching salt. You check "low-sodium," save, and the plan leans toward lower-sodium meals while you follow your provider's specific target.

## Foods commonly limited in pregnancy (general guidance)

PregnancyPal's "Foods to Avoid" section groups items as **High Risk** or **Limit**. As general guidance, many pregnancy resources commonly include:

- **High Risk** — raw or undercooked fish (such as sushi), unpasteurized ("raw") dairy and soft cheeses, raw or undercooked eggs, undercooked meat, and unwashed produce, because of foodborne-illness risk.
- **Limit** — high-mercury fish (such as shark, swordfish, king mackerel), deli or processed meats unless heated, excess caffeine, and added sugar.

These are general examples, not a personal medical plan. Your provider may add to or relax this list based on your health and where you live.

## Tips

- **Use the checkboxes for diets, the free-text for allergies.** Allergies that aren't in the standard list still belong in the Allergies field.
- **Re-check your restrictions each trimester.** Aversions and cravings shift; your plan should follow.
- **Heat deli meats until steaming** if you eat them — a common, simple safety step.
- **Wash fruits and vegetables well.** It's one of the easiest risk-reducers.
- **When in doubt, ask before you eat it.** A quick message to your provider beats guessing.

## Frequently asked questions

### Does PregnancyPal guarantee my plan is allergy-safe?

No. The plan tries to avoid foods you flag, but an app can't guarantee an allergen-free meal. Always read ingredient labels and check with restaurants yourself.

### Are the "Foods to Avoid" rules specific to me?

No. They're general pregnancy guidance shown for education. Your provider tailors food safety to your health, your region's guidelines, and any condition you have.

### Can I pick more than one restriction?

Yes. Check as many as apply — for example, vegetarian, gluten-free, and low-sodium together. The plan honors all of them when it regenerates.

### Why are some foods "High Risk" and others "Limit"?

High Risk foods carry a higher chance of foodborne illness and are generally avoided; Limit foods are usually fine in small amounts but worth keeping modest. The split is a general framing, not a personal verdict.

### What if a food I love is on the list?

Talk to your provider. Many "avoid" foods are about how they're prepared (raw vs. cooked) rather than the food itself, and your provider can give you a personal answer.

### Does changing restrictions delete my old plan?

It replaces the active plan with a regenerated one that matches your new inputs. You can change back anytime by editing preferences again.

## What this feature does NOT do

- **Does not give personalized medical food-safety advice** — the Foods to Avoid list is general education.
- **Does not guarantee allergen-free meals** — verify ingredients yourself.
- **Does not diagnose, treat, or predict** food intolerances, allergies, or pregnancy complications.
- **Does not account for your country's specific guidelines** or your individual medical history.
- **Does not replace a conversation with your provider** about what's safe for you to eat.

**The dietary guidance in PregnancyPal is for general education only and is not medical advice. Food-safety needs vary by person, pregnancy, and region — always consult your healthcare provider or a registered dietitian about your specific restrictions, allergies, and any food you're unsure about.**
