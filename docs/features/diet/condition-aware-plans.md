# Condition-aware plans

> **Condition-aware planning** describes an honest boundary in PregnancyPal: the app adapts your diet plan to the dietary restrictions, allergies, and calorie target you enter, but it does **not** auto-generate condition-specific medical diets for gestational diabetes, preeclampsia, anemia, or any other diagnosis. For a medical condition, your provider's or registered dietitian's plan comes first.

We want to be very clear and very supportive about this. If you've been diagnosed with a pregnancy condition, you might wish an app could just build the "right" diet for it. PregnancyPal can't do that safely, so it doesn't pretend to. Instead, it gives you tools to organize the plan your care team gives you.

This honesty is a feature, not a shortcoming. Medical nutrition for conditions like gestational diabetes or preeclampsia is precise, personal work that depends on your labs, your symptoms, and ongoing monitoring. That belongs with a qualified professional who knows your case — not an app working from a few preference toggles.

## What PregnancyPal can do around a condition

- **Reflect your restrictions and allergies** in the plan, so meals avoid foods you can't eat.
- **Match a calorie target you choose** with your provider's guidance.
- **Show general nutrition targets and foods-to-avoid guidance** for context.
- **Sit alongside your provider's plan** as an organization companion — a place to keep your preferences and track around your care team's instructions.
- **Pair with tracking tools** (weight, glucose, blood pressure, symptoms) so you have your logs ready for appointments.

## How to use the app when you have a condition

1. **Follow your care team's plan first.** Your provider or registered dietitian's medical diet is the authority — always.
2. **Enter your restrictions in PregnancyPal**: Dashboard → Diet Plan → "Edit Preferences," and record your real dietary limits and allergies.
3. **Set the calorie target your provider gave you** from the dropdown, if they specified one.
4. **Use the plan as a loose template**, then adjust meals to fit your medical guidance — for example, the carb targets your dietitian set for gestational diabetes.
5. **Track the relevant numbers** with PregnancyPal's logs (such as [glucose](../tracking/glucose) or [blood pressure](../tracking/blood-pressure)) so you can share them at visits.
6. **Bring your provider any mismatch.** If a suggested meal conflicts with your medical diet, follow your provider — not the app.

## Examples

- **Gestational diabetes** — Your dietitian gives you carb targets per meal. You keep that plan as your source of truth, enter your restrictions in PregnancyPal, and use its [glucose log](../tracking/glucose) to record readings for your appointments. You do not rely on the app to build the GDM diet.
- **Preeclampsia** — Your provider is monitoring your blood pressure and may adjust your care. You use PregnancyPal's [blood pressure log](../tracking/blood-pressure) to track readings and follow your provider's guidance on diet, rather than expecting an automatic low-sodium medical plan.
- **Iron-deficiency anemia** — Your provider prescribes iron and dietary changes. You note your restrictions in the app, check the general iron range under [nutrition targets](./nutrition-targets), and follow the supplement and diet plan your provider set.

## Tips

- **Let your care team lead.** The app organizes; your provider prescribes.
- **Keep your logs current.** Up-to-date glucose, weight, or blood-pressure entries make appointments more useful.
- **Use "Additional Notes" in Edit Preferences** to remind yourself of provider instructions you want reflected.
- **Don't treat any in-app meal as condition-approved.** It's a general template, not a medical diet.
- **Ask for a referral to a registered dietitian** if you have a condition and don't have one yet — they're the right expert for this.

## Frequently asked questions

### Can PregnancyPal make a gestational diabetes meal plan?

No. It does not generate condition-specific medical diets. It can reflect your restrictions and a calorie target, but your GDM plan must come from your provider or dietitian.

### Why won't the app build a plan for my condition?

Because doing it safely requires your labs, symptoms, and ongoing monitoring — things an app can't responsibly assess. Getting it wrong could harm you or your baby, so the app stays in its lane.

### Whose plan should I follow if they conflict?

Always your provider's or registered dietitian's plan. If a PregnancyPal suggestion conflicts with their guidance, follow them and ignore the app's suggestion.

### Is the app still useful if I have a condition?

Yes — as an organizer. Record your restrictions, set the calorie target your provider gave you, and use the tracking logs to bring data to appointments.

### Does it adjust for twins or multiples?

No. The plan reflects only the preferences you enter and does not adjust for multiples or any medical situation.

### Can I show my logs to my dietitian?

Yes. You can export your tracked data in a health report PDF to share at a visit — see [health export PDF](../reporting/health-export-pdf).

## What this feature does NOT do

- **Does not create condition-specific medical diets** for gestational diabetes, preeclampsia, anemia, or any diagnosis.
- **Does not diagnose, treat, manage, or predict** any pregnancy condition.
- **Does not override or replace** your provider's or registered dietitian's plan.
- **Does not interpret your labs, symptoms, or readings** to adjust your diet.
- **Does not adjust for multiples** or any individual medical circumstance.

**PregnancyPal does not provide condition-specific medical nutrition and is not a substitute for professional care. If you have a pregnancy condition, your healthcare provider's or registered dietitian's plan takes precedence — always follow their guidance and consult them before making any diet change.**
