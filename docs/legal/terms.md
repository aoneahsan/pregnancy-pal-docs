# Terms summary

> **Terms summary** — a plain-language overview of the agreement you accept when you use PregnancyPal, including acceptable use and the all-important medical disclaimer.

These are the ground rules for using PregnancyPal, written in everyday language so you actually know what you're agreeing to. The short version: use the app responsibly, treat the community kindly, never mistake it for medical care, and understand it's offered free and as-is.

**This page is a summary, not the binding agreement.** The legally binding terms are the app's own page at [pregnancypal.aoneahsan.com/terms](https://pregnancypal.aoneahsan.com/terms). If anything here and the binding terms ever seem to differ, the app's `/terms` page is the one that governs.

## Acceptable use

When you use PregnancyPal, you agree to use it lawfully and reasonably. In plain terms, that means:

- **Use it for its purpose** — tracking, learning, and staying organized through pregnancy or trying to conceive.
- **Don't misuse the service** — no attempts to break, overload, reverse-engineer, or gain unauthorized access to the app or other people's accounts.
- **Keep your account yours** — it's tied to your Google sign-in; don't impersonate others or share access in ways that put data at risk.
- **Respect others** — anywhere the app lets you interact with other people (such as community or cohort features), be kind and follow the community guidelines.

If you seriously or repeatedly break these rules, access to the service may be limited or ended.

## Community guidelines

Where PregnancyPal includes any community or shared spaces, those spaces run on basic respect: no harassment, no hateful or harmful content, no spam, and no sharing of others' private information. The full, binding community expectations live with the app's [terms](https://pregnancypal.aoneahsan.com/terms). Pregnancy is a vulnerable, emotional time for many people — the bar is simple: be the kind of presence you'd want to find there.

## No medical advice (please read)

This is the most important part. **PregnancyPal is an educational and organizational companion — it is not a medical service.** It does not diagnose, treat, cure, or prevent any condition. It does not monitor your pregnancy, predict outcomes, or replace the judgment of a trained professional. Reminders, content, and tracking tools are there to help you stay informed and organized, not to make clinical decisions for you.

Never delay seeking care, or disregard advice from your provider, because of something you read or tracked in PregnancyPal. If you think you may have a medical emergency, contact emergency services right away.

## Free, and provided "as-is"

PregnancyPal is offered **free of charge**. Because of that, it's provided on an **"as-is" and "as-available" basis** — there's no guarantee it will be uninterrupted, error-free, or perfectly accurate, and the developer isn't liable for issues that arise from using it, to the extent the law allows. Features may change, pause, or be discontinued over time. The binding terms spell out these limitations in full.

## The information you enter is yours to verify

You decide what to track, and you're responsible for the accuracy of what you enter. PregnancyPal stores and organizes your information faithfully, but it doesn't independently confirm it, and it doesn't make decisions on your behalf. Treat reminders and content as helpful prompts, not instructions — your provider's guidance always takes priority over anything the app shows. If a date, measurement, or piece of content ever seems off, check it with your provider rather than relying on the app alone.

## Frequently asked questions

### Do I have to pay anything?

No. PregnancyPal is free. There are no premium tiers gating the core experience. If that ever changes, it would be made clear in the app first.

### Can the developer change these terms?

Yes — terms can be updated over time. The binding version is always the live page at [pregnancypal.aoneahsan.com/terms](https://pregnancypal.aoneahsan.com/terms). Continuing to use the app means accepting the current terms.

### What happens if I break the rules?

Misusing the service or harming others can lead to limits on or termination of your access. The binding terms describe this in more detail.

### How do I stop using PregnancyPal?

You can stop anytime. To remove your data as well, delete your account from inside the app — see the [delete-account](../features/account/delete-account) page for what's removed and what's retained.
